There are different types of physical therapy exercises that may be prescribed for you depending on your specific condition. These exercises may include:

  • exercises to improve strength
  • exercises to improve range of motion
  • flexibility exercises
  • balance and proprioception exercises
  • functional mobility exercises
  • cardiorespiratory exercises
  • exercises for vertigo and dizziness

Exercises

Wall Pushup

Wall push-ups allow for a gradual progression of upper body strength and load-bearing exercises, making them suitable for individuals with limited strength or recovering from injuries.

Get started
Shoulder-blade

Shoulder blade exercises help improve scapular stability and strength, by targeting the muscles around the shoulder blades. These exercises enhance posture, shoulder function and reduce the risk of shoulder injuries.

Get started
Toe reach

Toe reach exercises improve flexibility, balance. By reaching towards the toes, individuals engage the muscles of the lower back, hips, and legs, promoting range of motion and strengthening the core and lower body muscles.

Get started
Straigtening back

Straightening back exercises improve posture and alleviate back pain. It strengthens the muscles of the back. By focusing on straightening the back, individuals can reduce stress on the spine and enhance postural alignment.

Get started
Hand exercise using dumbbell

Hand exercises using dumbbells helps strengthen the muscles of the hand, wrist and forearm. These exercises enhance coordination, allowing to regain control and precision in hand movements.
